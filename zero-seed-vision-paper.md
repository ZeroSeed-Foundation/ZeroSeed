# Zero Seed: Vision Paper

*Vision paper to be written. Below are potential considerations to inform it.*

---

## Potential considerations

- **Selection without universals.** How do you identify Seed Keepers and node keepers through local selection while acknowledging the fragility of any single standard? What keeps selection from hardening into a new orthodoxy?
- **Common ground without agreement.** The soccer-field idea implies a shared surface that doesn't require shared beliefs. What counts as "common ground" in practice? How do you maintain it without defining it so tightly that it becomes another ideology?
- **Staying acyclical.** The layer is meant to sit beneath politics, ideology, and nation. How do you avoid being pulled into partisan or ideological fights? What governance or norms keep the layer from becoming just another pole in the same game?
- **Scaling without centralizing.** Local → regional → global suggests coordination across scales. How do you preserve local diversity and avoid a single global "design" while still enabling coordination on existential risks? *Answer:* The application is decentralized, localized, and regionalized on decentralized nodes; it does not dictate how initiatives are organized or which tech stacks they use. It aggregates proposals and projects that serve existence and presents them as opportunities to the right people and resources—so coordination happens through matching and visibility, not through a single global design.
- **Resilience vs. efficiency.** If Zero Seed optimizes for resilience and not always for optimal efficiency, what does that mean for day-to-day choices (redundancy, diversity of approaches, speed of decision-making)?
- **Interface with the mainstream.** The aim is to mitigate collapse of the mainstream, not replace it. How do you relate to existing institutions—states, markets, NGOs—without being absorbed or instrumentalized by them?
- **What "support over the network" means.** Seed Keepers can ask for support. What forms does that take (material, coordination, information, legitimacy), and what are the limits so the network doesn't become a dependency trap?
- **Existential risk at each scale.** "Existential" may mean something different locally (e.g. community survival) vs. globally (e.g. species-level). How do you hold both without one framing colonizing the other?
- **Other layers as a ledger of conscious Seed Keepers.** Zero Seed supports what lies beneath; the other layers could be understood as a ledger of conscious Seed Keepers. What does that imply for how those layers relate to the Zero Seed layer, and how the ledger is maintained or made visible?
- **What the coordination layer is.** The Zero Seed application is decentralized, localized, and regionalized, hosted on decentralized nodes. When we say it is a coordination layer, we mean: a place where anyone can propose policy proposals or projects that **serve existence** (environment, food, clothing, shelter). These can be brought into being by connecting the proposal with the right people and resources—helping it move from a seed of an idea to something tangible. The platform does not dictate how these initiatives are organized; different projects can lean on different technology stacks per their convenience. In that sense the Zero Seed application layer aggregates projects, proposals, and initiatives and presents them as opportunities to the right people.
